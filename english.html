Introduction to Human Rights

  • Rights inherent to all human beings
  • Apply regardless of race, religion, nationality
  • Universal Declaration of Human Rights adopted in 1948
  • All humans are born free and equal in dignity

Right to Life and Security

  • Article 3 of the UDHR
  • Right to life, liberty, and security
  • Foundation of all other rights
  • Protection from violence and threats

Freedom from Slavery and Torture

  • No one shall be held in slavery
  • No torture or cruel punishment
  • Modern slavery includes trafficking and forced labor
  • These rights are absolute

Right to Justice

  • Right to a fair and public trial
  • Presumption of innocence
  • Equality before the law
  • Prevents abuse of power

Right of Accessibility

  • Ability to fully participate in society
  • Accessible buildings and transport
  • Access to education and information
  • Inclusive society without barriers

Right of Religion

  • Freedom of thought and belief
  • Right to follow any religion or none
  • Right to practice publicly or privately
  • Protects minority religions

Conclusion

  • Human rights protect dignity
  • They are the foundation of justice and peace
  • Everyone must respect the rights of others
  • "To deny people their human rights is to challenge their very humanity." — Nelson Mandela
